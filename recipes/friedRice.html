Fried Rice Recipe

Description:

Fire Rice (Korean Buldak Bokkeum-bap) is an explosively spicy, smoky fried rice tossed in a sweet-and-savory gochujang sauce. It features tender chicken and crispy vegetables, all finished with melted cheese and a crunchy seaweed sprinkle to balance the heat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white rice (cold, day-old preferred)
  • 1 boneless, skinless chicken thigh (diced small)
  • 1/2 small onion (diced)
  • 1/2 cup cabbage (shredded) or frozen mixed veggies
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) – reduce for less heat
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar or honey
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tbsp green onions (chopped, for garnish)
  • 1 sheet roasted seaweed (crushed, for garnish)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps to Prepare:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, and sugar/honey into a smooth sauce. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until golden and fully done (3-4 mins).
  3. Add the onion, cabbage, and garlic. Stir-fry for 2 minutes until the veggies soften slightly.
  4. Add the cold rice and the prepared sauce to the pan. Break up any clumps and stir-fry vigorously for 2-3 minutes until the rice is evenly coated and piping hot.
  5. Drizzle the sesame oil over the rice and toss one last time. Turn off the heat.
  6. (Optional for extra flavor) Sprinkle the mozzarella over the top, cover the pan with a lid for 1 minute until melted, or skip to serve immediately.
  7. Plate the rice, top with green onions, crushed seaweed, and serve hot!
