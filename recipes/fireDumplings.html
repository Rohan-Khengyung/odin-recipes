Fire Dumplings (Buldak Mandu) Recipe

Description:

Fire Dumplings are a crispy, pan-fried twist on Korean "fire chicken" (buldak). Filled with a spicy, savory mix of chicken and vegetables, they're pan-fried until golden and often topped with melted cheese to balance the intense heat

Ingredients:

For the Filling & Sauce

  • 1 cup ground chicken (or pork/beef)
  • 1/2 scup finely chopped cabbage
  • 2 green onions, finely sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) – reduce for less heat
  • 1 tbsp sugar or honey
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar

For the Wrappers & Cooking:

  • 30 round dumpling wrappers (or use store-bought)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil (for pan-frying)
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese (optional)
  • Sesame seeds & crushed seaweed (for garnish)

Steps to Prepare:

  1. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, and rice vinegar until smooth
  2. Make the filling: In a separate bowl, combine the ground chicken, cabbage, green onions, garlic, and ginger. Pour the sauce over the filling and mix well
  3. Fill the dumplings: Place about 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of each wrapper. Wet the edges with water, fold, and seal tightly.
  4. Pan-fry: Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the dumplings in a single layer and cook for 2-3 minutes until the bottoms are golden and crispy
  5. Add steam: Pour ¼ cup of water into the pan, cover immediately with a lid, and steam for 4-5 minutes until the filling is cooked through and the water evaporates.
  6. (Optional) Add cheese: Sprinkle mozzarella over the dumplings, cover for 1 minute until melted
  7. Serve: Garnish with sesame seeds and crushed seaweed. Serve hot with pickled radish or a cool dipping sauce!
